WHY WE STARTED

The era of disruptive innovation is here with us and we are pleased to be a part of generation that seeks to affect humanity positively. In today’s era of big data, we have observed how companies have managed to develop their business models on data analytics foundations. The likes of Facebook, and Google have since dominated the innovative market by implementing an analytic-driven culture in all aspects of operations for both products and services. Thus, the data economy comprising of cloud, IoT, AI and data, is now gaining interest from large organizations, who have realized the power of big data and analytics and seek to harness its capabilities for corporate and societal development. The shift from Analytics 1.0 (Traditional Analytics) to 3.0 has been an era most data lovers have been waiting for- the ability to utilize data analytics in all aspects of an organizations operations, and incorporate data intelligence into its products and services, resulting in significant business insights. Join us on this riveting journey as we discover the power of data.

Why Word Cloud?
Word clouds help in identifying the crucial points in the business and improving their opportunities. They are great for feedback & research analysis to bring deep insights.They can instantly reveal the indispensable information & be engaging to the viewer. These compelling pictorial images enhance the text mining process, make an impact & are easy to understand. It is widely used by researchers, educators, analysts, journalists, politicians and social media sites.Word cloud can be generated using various online applications. Humans are generally strong at visual analytics. That is part of the reason that these have become so popular. What follows are a variety of alternatives for constructing word clouds with your text. First, Let’s focus on Python and R.
Using R:
The libraries required for generating word clouds are tm, snowballC, wordcloud. Once they are installed, the text file is loaded to generate a word cloud. The features(color, font, excluding stop words, orientation) of the graphical representation can be modified according to the requirements. The input given in R is a text file( a greek mythology, who wouldn’t love it!), the word cloud generated gists the huge mythology.
Using Python:
The packages used in python are Pandas, Numpy, Word cloud, PIL, wikipedia. It generated different word clouds by extracting data from social media (linkedin) as inputs. The inputs are given in the form of strings, text file, wikipedia and CSV to generate the word cloud visualizations. Implications of word cloud: Word clouds aren’t useful in all the scenarios, especially when the content is not optimized for keywords. There could be another visualization techniques that could be more useful. YES! That’s correct, It’s crucial to identify which visualization technique works best for the data you’re working with. Try it yourself!
